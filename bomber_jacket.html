BOMBER JACKET TREND ACROSS THE WORLD

What is a Bomber Jacket?

Bomber Jacket, also known as a flight jacket or bombardier jacket, is a garment that was originally created for use by pilots. Since World War I when it made the first appreance, the bomber jacket has grown in popularity throughout the world.

In the Past

Since the majority of airplanes flown in World War-I did not feature enclosed cockpits, pilots had to wear a jacket that would keep them warm while also giving them the mobility to fly the plane. In 1915, the Royal Flying Corps pilots began wearing long leather coats and the style passed on to the French, American and Belgian military. By the time the United States entered World War I in 1917, the government had established the Aviation Clothing Board to provide comfortable and warm leather jackets to all pilots. The original bomber jacket featured a high wraparound collar that protected the pilot's neck from the wind, zipper closures and snug waists and cuffs to keep the pilot insulated and warm. In addition, the jackets were lined with fur.

Today

Today bomber jacket has become a symbol of style, power and leadership with everyone from presidents to executives sporting the garment. No longer confined by its military roots, its design has adapted with new stylings to the iconic look. The bomber jacket is now a true fashion essential, one found draped across the shoulders of nearly everyone who gives any thought to what they wear.

Where is Bomber Jacket Trending towards?

Trends around the world

2016 is considered as the year of the Bomber Jacket. How the bombers spread out across time and places around the world becomes the main interest here.

How frequent people search for the term "bomber jacket" is a good indicator of the popularity of that fashion item. Google Trends is a tool that shows how often a particular search-term is entered across various regions of the world, and in various languages.

Search volume data was retrieved from Google Trends for the time period from Nov 2010 to Mar 2016 for 1933 different cities. 8 different languages (as shown in the word cloud on the right) were used to pull data for different regions. The data is then normalized using search categories.

Fashion Statement or Just Another Accesory to Stay Warm?

Bomber jacket was initially designed for pilots to keep themselves warm. Therefore the primary question we addressed was if that was still true.

We performed analysis to determine if an online query for bomber jacket was to make a potential purchase for fashion statement or to make a potential purchase as an accessory to stay warm.

Following was the hypothesis we addressed

Null Hypothesis (H0): Online Queries for Bomber Jacket (from Google Trend) is towards a potential purchase to keep warm

Alternate Hypothesis (H1): Online Queries for Bomber Jacket (from Google Trend) is towards a potential purchase for fashion statement In order to answer this question, we divided countries/regions/cities into two groups: cold and hot. Please refer to Appendix-2 for details on weights. The average winter temperature was retrieved from Wikipedia as base to determine weights for classification.

Each country/region/city was given a weight based on the average winter temperature. This formed the basic to determine how to categorize. Such categorization allowed us to compare the number of queries. A person querying a bomber jacket from a "hot" country/region/city is obviously shopping for its fashion value adding validity to our alternate hypothesis.

Conclusion

Analysis results threw suprising results. Contrary to the general perception the trends for cold and hot cities almost match each other. Surprisingly, even though cold cities show larger trend, hot cities follow a similar trend pattern.

The result also indicates that bomber jacket is no longer a seasonal item. The searches are consistently high all-year-round, meaning it's about style as much as it is utility.

Appendix-2: Weight Hot/Cold Countries

The formula to calculate the weight to differentiate hot/cold countries is given below.

Weight = (Average Winter temp in farenheit)/(sum of all average winter temperatures for countries used in Analysis) * (total number of entries)

Based on a threshold we could classify the country as hot or cold.
